About Ninh Binh

Ninh Binh is a province in northern Vietnam, located about 100 km south of Hanoi. With spectacular natural scenery, Ninh Binh is known as "Halong Bay on land" and is one of the must-visit destinations when coming to Vietnam.

Geographic Location

Ninh Binh is located in northern Vietnam, coordinates: 20°18'00" North, 105°52'00" East. The province has an area of 1,380 km² and borders Thanh Hoa, Hoa Binh, Ha Nam and Hai Phong provinces.

History

Ninh Binh has a long and rich history. It was the capital of many Vietnamese feudal dynasties. The ancient city of Hoa Lu was built from the 10th century and is where many great Vietnamese kings were born.

During the French colonial period and French resistance war, Ninh Binh was an important resistance center. Hoa Lu and surrounding areas still bear traces of those battles.

Climate

Ninh Binh has a subtropical humid climate with four distinct seasons:

  • Spring (3-5): Pleasant weather, temperature 20-25°C
  • Summer (6-8): Hot and humid, temperature 28-32°C
  • Autumn (9-11): Cool, temperature 22-26°C
  • Winter (12-2): Cold, temperature 15-20°C

Population

Ninh Binh has a population of about 900,000 people. The residents are mainly Vietnamese, speak Vietnamese, and follow Buddhism and folk religions.

Economy

Ninh Binh's economy mainly depends on:

  • Agriculture: Growing rice, vegetables, and fruits, mainly in flat areas
  • Tourism: A major economic sector of the province
  • Industry: Agricultural processing and construction materials manufacturing

Culture and Traditions

Ninh Binh has a rich cultural heritage with many traditional festivals:

  • Trang An Festival: Held annually in the Trang An area
  • Hoa Lu Festival: Honoring ancient kings
  • Autumn Festival: Celebrating the autumn season in rural areas

Importance of Tourism

Tourism plays a vital role in Ninh Binh's socio-economic development. In recent years, the tourism industry has grown rapidly, attracting millions of visitors from home and abroad.

Tourist attractions such as Trang An, Hoa Lu, Mua Caves, and Tam Coc have become famous not only nationally but also internationally.
